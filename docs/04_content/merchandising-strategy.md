# Merchandising Strategy & Catalog Architecture

*Owner: Merchandising Strategist*

## 1. Merchandising Philosophy: "Curated Restraint"
We do not sell a bloated catalog. The assortment must feel edited, intentional, and taste-led. It is a gallery, not a warehouse. We guide the customer; we do not overwhelm them with endless, slightly varied options.

## 2. Catalog Taxonomy (Phase 1 Launch)

The structure must be ruthlessly simple, categorized by room and distinct collections.

*   **Seating**
    *   Sofas & Sectionals
    *   Accent Chairs & Lounge Chairs
    *   Ottomans & Poufs
*   **Tables**
    *   Coffee Tables
    *   Side Tables
    *   Dining Tables
*   **Storage & Media**
    *   Media Consoles
    *   Credenzas
*   **Bundles & Styled Sets** (Crucial for high AOV and stylistic guidance)
    *   The Living Room Anchor Set (Sofa + Coffee Table + Rug/Lamp)
    *   The Executive Office Set

## 3. The "Hero" Product Strategy
Every category must have a defined "Hero" piece. The Hero piece receives:
*   Disproportionate visual real estate on the PLP (spanning two columns).
*   Deep-dive editorial content (a dedicated "The Making Of" section on its PDP).
*   Priority placement in Lookbook environments.

## 4. Cross-Selling & Styling Rules
We avoid algorithmic, disjointed "You Might Also Like" carousels.

*   **Rule 1: Contextual Matching.** If a user is viewing a chunky, low-profile sofa, cross-sells should feature complimentary items (e.g., a low, dark-wood coffee table), not another sofa.
*   **Rule 2: "Pairs Well With."** This module must be manually curated by the styling team or driven by strict aesthetic tagging (e.g., tag: *Wabi-Sabi*, tag: *Mid-Century Modern*).
*   **Rule 3: Bundling Prompts.** On high-ticket items, subtly propose the Bundled Set in the cart drawer. *(Note: Incentives like "save 10% on shipping" must be driven by configurable commerce rules, not hardcoded text.)*

## 5. Product Naming Convention
Names must reflect the premium, somewhat architectural nature of the brand.
*   *Avoid:* overly literal or cheap-sounding names (e.g., "The Comfy Couch", "Lagos Sofa").
*   *Adopt:* Short, evocative, somewhat abstract or globally-resonant names (e.g., "The Noka Sofa", "The Veda Console", "The Alta Dining Chair").

## 6. Managing Out-of-Stock and Custom Intent
Because premium Nigerian furniture often relies on imported fabrics or specific lumber yields:
*   **Never hide OOP items.** They act as social proof of desirability.
*   **The Waitlist:** Change "Add to Cart" to "Join Waitlist."
*   **Custom Intent (Bespoke):** If an item is out of stock in Leather but available in Velvet, clearly badge the Leather option with "Available via Custom Order." *(Note: Lead times, waitlists, and required deposit percentages must be dynamically pulled from configurable business rules, not hardcoded into the UI.)*

## 7. The "Shop by Room" (Lookbook) Strategy
High-net-worth buyers often want to buy an entire "look" rather than piecing it together.
*   The Lookbook sections must feature fully 3D-rendered or photographed hero rooms.
*   Interactive hotspots on the image must seamlessly pop out mini-PDP cards containing the Add to Cart button, preventing the user from leaving the inspiration flow.
